# Dashboard Story — Executive Sales & Profitability Review

**Audience:** Retail Leadership Team
**Period covered:** January 2024 – December 2025

## Executive Summary
Over the last two years the business generated **₹217M in sales** and **₹33.3M in profit** (15.3% overall margin) across 4,200 orders. The headline story is not a growth or decline story — it's a **mix story**. Total sales are fairly stable year over year with a repeating seasonal dip each spring, but profitability is being pulled in opposite directions by two forces: a highly profitable Technology category and acquisition channel mix on one side, and a structurally low-margin, high-return Furniture category on the other. The dashboard's filters let leadership isolate any region, segment, category, or time window to see this mix effect directly.

## What Is Performing Well
- **Technology** is the profit engine: 18.2% margin and ₹28.0M of the company's ₹33.3M total profit comes from this single category, led by Copiers, Accessories, Phones, and Machines, all sitting around 18% margin.
- **East region** converts sales into profit most efficiently (15.6% margin), even though it isn't the largest region by volume — a sign of disciplined pricing/discounting there.
- **Organic acquisition** is both the largest channel (₹88.8M sales) and the most efficient, contributing the majority of total profit without paid acquisition cost.
- Customer ratings stay healthy (averaging ~4.0/5) even as delivery time increases, suggesting the core buying experience is solid.

## What Is Underperforming
- **Furniture** is the clear laggard: 6.9% margin versus 15–18% elsewhere, with **Tables** and **Bookcases** the two weakest sub-categories in the entire catalog (~5.7% margin).
- **West region** is the weakest performer on both sales and margin — it isn't catastrophic, but it lags every other region on both dimensions simultaneously, which is unusual.
- **Home Office** customers return orders at 5.7%, noticeably above Consumer and Corporate (~4%), without buying meaningfully more — that gap is pure friction.

## Risks Visible in the Data
- **Discount creep**: margin drops from ~20% on lightly-discounted orders to ~6.5% on orders discounted 25%+. Deep discounting is close to erasing the value of the sale.
- **Channel concentration**: heavy reliance on Organic for the bulk of profit is efficient today but is a single point of dependence if organic visibility shifts.
- **Returns are concentrated, not random**: Bookcases, Furnishings, and Chairs together account for a disproportionate share of all returns — this is a recurring, identifiable cluster rather than scattered noise.

## Opportunities Visible in the Data
- **Referral channel is under-leveraged**: it's the smallest channel today (₹21.2M) despite referrals typically being a low-cost, high-trust acquisition source — there's clear room to grow this deliberately.
- **East's playbook is replicable**: because East already proves higher margin is achievable without sacrificing meaningful sales volume, its pricing/discount discipline is a candidate model to test in West.
- **Shipping speed is not the lever leadership might assume**: return rates stay roughly flat (4.2%–4.9%) regardless of delivery delay, which frees the business to prioritize spend elsewhere (e.g., product quality) rather than over-investing in faster fulfillment.

## Recommended Business Actions
1. Set a discount approval threshold (e.g., anything above ~20% requires sign-off) to protect margin on the categories most exposed to discount erosion.
2. Run a focused review of Tables, Bookcases, Chairs, and Furnishings — the same sub-categories show up as both lowest-margin and highest-return, so one investigation (product quality, listing accuracy, freight cost) likely addresses two problems at once.
3. Pilot a structured referral program to grow the most cost-efficient under-used channel.
4. Use East's regional performance as an internal benchmark/playbook for West.
5. Build a spring promotional calendar around the recurring April–May sales dip instead of treating each year's dip as a surprise.

## Limitations of the Dashboard
- The dataset covers two fiscal years (2024–2025) only; it cannot speak to longer-term multi-year trends or pre-2024 baselines.
- Profit and margin figures are as recorded in the source data; the dashboard does not have visibility into fixed costs, overhead, or marketing spend by channel, so "channel profit" reflects gross order profit only, not fully-loaded acquisition cost.
- Return reasons (why an order was returned) are not captured in the data — the dashboard shows *that* returns are concentrated in certain categories, not *why*.
- Campaign channel has some missing values (~0.6% of orders), which are excluded from channel-level totals.

## Suggested Next Analysis
- A root-cause review of returns for Bookcases, Furnishings, and Chairs (e.g., customer return-reason codes, product reviews) to convert this dashboard's "what" into an actionable "why."
- A cost-to-serve analysis for Furniture that incorporates freight/logistics cost, to confirm whether the low margin is pricing-driven or shipping-cost-driven.
- A referral-program pilot with a defined test period, tracked against the channel benchmarks shown here.
